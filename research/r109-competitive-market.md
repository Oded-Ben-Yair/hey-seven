### 1.1 Quick Custom Intelligence (QCI) — MARKET LEADER

**Overview**: San Diego-based, co-founded by Dr. Ralph Thomas (CEO) and Andrew Cardno. The dominant casino analytics platform globally.

**Scale & Traction**:
- 300+ casino resorts across North America, Australia, Canada, Asia, Latin America
- Manages $42 billion in annual gross gaming revenue
- 100 new customer engagements in 2025 (40%+ compound annual growth)
- Offices: San Diego, Las Vegas, St. Louis, Denver, Dallas, Phoenix
- Planning **nine-figure R&D investment** in 2026

**Key Products**:
- **Chatalytics**: Conversational/agentic AI tool launched Nov 2024, adopted by 400+ properties ("most successful product launch in QCI's history"). Enables non-technical managers to build dashboards via natural language — "a project that would have taken 2-3 months built in under one day"
- **QCI Host Nimble Edition**: Launched at ICE Barcelona 2026 (Jan 2026) — player development tool for smaller teams, progressive web app, mobile-first
- **VizExplorer**: Casino analytics and operational intelligence platform (slots, marketing, player development, floor performance)
- **QCI Player / QCI Customer / AGI56**: Additional platform components
- **QCI Research**: Launched Aug 2025 as part of broader engagement model

**Funding**: Secured eight-figure investment from Curve Partners (Jul 2025). Appears to be primarily self-funding growth. No disclosed total raise.

**Competitive Assessment**: QCI is the gorilla in the room. Their "Chatalytics" conversational AI is the closest product to Hey Seven's approach, but it's a dashboard query tool for casino operators, NOT a guest-facing autonomous host. QCI empowers HUMAN hosts with better data — Hey Seven REPLACES the human host for digital tasks. Different product category entirely.

**Key Quote**: "AI accountability" over experimental AI adoption (Jan 2026 announcement).

### 1.3 OPTX (OPTX Solutions, LLC)

**Overview**: Las Vegas-based, founded 2019 by Brooke Fiumara and Tom Rafferty (co-CEOs, former casino operators).

**Scale**: Serves casinos from single properties to large enterprises across North America. Customers include Soboba, Viejas, Graton, Red Hawk, Spokane Tribe.

**Funding**: Described as "unfunded" by Tracxn (2026). Crunchbase placeholder only. Organic growth model.

**Relation to Acres Technology**: No direct partnership or acquisition found. Both appear independently at gaming events. Acres Technology (Foundation CMS, cashless tables) raised $29.6M in 2023, separate entity focused on casino floor systems and loyalty.

**Competitive Assessment**: OPTX is a strong analytics platform but squarely in the "tools for human hosts" category. Their Future Player Value prediction is impressive but outputs to dashboards, not to autonomous conversations. No guest-facing capability.

### 1.5 Cosmopolitan Rose AI

**Overview**: AI chatbot at The Cosmopolitan of Las Vegas, launched 2017. "Resident Mischief-Maker" personality — sassy, flirty, witty.

**Capabilities**:
- Handles 80% of guest queries instantly (20% escalated to humans)
- Boosts guest satisfaction by 33%, spend by 30-37%
- SMS-based (text "HiRose" to 447673)
- Room service, dining recs, show tickets, VIP access, skip-line perks, games
- Integrated with MGM Rewards loyalty program (Jul 2024)

**2025-2026 Status**: Still actively featured (Nov 2025 fact sheet, live site as of Mar 2026). No major updates announced. No significant social media mentions.

**Competitive Assessment**: Rose is the original casino AI chatbot and the most well-known case study. But it's:
1. **Single-property** (Cosmopolitan only, not a platform)
2. **Rule-based** with NLP layer (not agentic AI)
3. **Concierge, not host** (answers questions, doesn't build relationships or manage player development)
4. **No player development** (doesn't do comp decisions, VIP identification, or proactive outreach)
5. **No profiling** (doesn't extract guest data for host team handoff)
Rose is the benchmark for "AI in casinos" but is a fundamentally different product than Hey Seven.
