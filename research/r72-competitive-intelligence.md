## 4. How Competitors Handle Difficult Interactions

### Angry Callers
- **PolyAI**: Transfers to human after detecting elevated frustration. No de-escalation.
- **Industry standard**: Escalation to human supervisor. No AI-native de-escalation.
- **Hey Seven opportunity**: HEART framework (Hear, Empathize, Apologize, Resolve, Thank), graduated by frustration level, with empathetic responses before any service action.

### Drunk Callers
- **Current state**: No AI product specifically handles drunk/impaired communication
- **Industry**: Human agents use judgment, sometimes refuse service
- **Hey Seven opportunity**: Pattern recognition for impaired texting (typos, ALL CAPS, emotional swings, late-night timing) with appropriate response adaptation

### Problem Gambling
- **PolyAI**: No detection or intervention capability
- **QCall/BetHarmony**: Basic KYC only (age verification)
- **Industry**: Reliance on self-exclusion lists and human observation
- **Hey Seven opportunity**: Five-layer pre-LLM guardrails (prompt injection, responsible gaming, age verification, BSA/AML, patron privacy) with state-specific helpline routing

## 5. Regulatory & Certification Landscape

### Current AI Gaming Certifications
- **GLI (Gaming Laboratories International)**: Tests gaming equipment/software. No specific AI agent certification yet.
- **BMM Testlabs**: Similar to GLI. Focused on game fairness, not conversational AI.
- **State gaming commissions**: Nevada Gaming Control Board, New Jersey DGE, etc. regulate casino operations but have limited AI-specific rules.
- **Responsible gaming organizations**: National Council on Problem Gambling (NCPG), International Center for Responsible Gaming (ICRG) provide guidelines but not certifications.

### Emerging Regulatory Requirements
- **Transparency**: Several states considering requirements that AI must identify itself as non-human
- **Problem gambling**: Increased pressure to detect and intervene using technology
- **Data privacy**: CCPA and state-specific regulations apply to guest data collection
- **BSA/AML**: Anti-money laundering requirements extend to AI-assisted patron communication
- **TCPA**: SMS/text communication requires explicit opt-in compliance

### Hey Seven's Regulatory Advantage
- Purpose-built compliance guardrails (5 layers, pre-LLM)
- State-specific helpline routing (not generic national number)
- TCPA compliance built into SMS module
- BSA/AML pattern detection
- Audit logging for all guardrail triggers
- This regulatory infrastructure is a significant moat — PolyAI has none of it for gambling-specific compliance

## 8. Implications for Hey Seven Agent

### Competitive Positioning
Hey Seven occupies a **genuinely uncontested category**: the ongoing AI casino host relationship via text. Key positioning:

1. **Not competing with PolyAI** — complementary. PolyAI handles inbound calls, Hey Seven handles ongoing VIP relationship via SMS/text
2. **"Where PolyAI stops, Hey Seven starts"** — PolyAI automates the transaction, Hey Seven manages the relationship
3. **Regulatory moat**: Five-layer guardrails, state-specific compliance, responsible gaming detection. No competitor has this
4. **Emotional intelligence moat**: Sarcasm detection, sentiment analysis, emotional context guides, conversation dynamics adaptation. No competitor approaches this
5. **Cultural intelligence moat**: Generational language adaptation, cultural gambling norms, bilingual handling

### Key Risks to Monitor
1. **PolyAI expanding to text/SMS**: Most likely competitive threat. Their Agent Studio could add SMS channel
2. **Casino operators building in-house**: Caesars/MGM have data science teams; custom build risk
3. **Regulation changes**: AI identification requirements, data privacy tightening
4. **Market education**: Operators still need to understand why text-based AI host > just a reservation bot
5. **PolyAI acquisition of SMS/relationship company**: Would close the gap quickly

### Near-Term Opportunities
1. **Partner with PolyAI**: Not compete. PolyAI handles inbound calls, Hey Seven handles ongoing text relationship. Same casino, different channels.
2. **Target PolyAI's existing customers**: Golden Nugget, Caesars, Peppermill already have AI trust. Pitch the relationship layer on top.
3. **Lead with compliance**: Responsible gaming guardrails are a regulatory requirement that no other AI product provides. This is a buying requirement, not a nice-to-have.
