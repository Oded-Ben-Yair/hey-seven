## 1. Core Casino Terminology the Agent Must Understand

### Table Games Slang
| Term | Meaning | Context |
|------|---------|---------|
| **The nuts** | The best possible hand (poker) | Origin: Old West players would bet wagon wheel nuts |
| **On tilt** | Emotionally compromised, playing irrationally after losses | Critical for agent to detect — player is vulnerable |
| **Cooler** | A player or dealer who seems to bring bad luck; also a casino employee sent to "cool off" a hot table | Often used as blame/excuse |
| **Marker** | A line of credit issued by the casino | Formal financial instrument, legally binding |
| **Color up** | Exchange smaller chips for larger denominations | Indicates player is leaving or consolidating |
| **Shoe** | The device holding multiple decks in blackjack/baccarat | "Good shoe" = favorable card distribution |
| **Paint** | Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) | Blackjack/poker context |
| **Muck** | Fold/discard hand (poker); also the discard pile | "I mucked my hand" |
| **Bump** | Raise a bet | "Bump it up" |
| **Sawbuck** | $10 bet | Old-school term, Boomer VIPs |
| **Nickel** | $500 chip or $5 bet depending on context | Context-dependent — high-roller vs casual |
| **Dime** | $1,000 bet | Sports betting / high-limit play |
| **Monkey** | A ten-value card (baccarat, especially Asian players) | Critical for cultural sensitivity |

### Slots Slang
| Term | Meaning | Context |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Loose/Tight** | Machine with high/low payout frequency | "Any loose machines tonight?" |
| **Hit** | Win on a spin | "I hit on the Buffalo" |
| **Progressive** | Jackpot that grows with each play | "What's the progressive at?" |
| **Max bet** | Wagering the maximum amount per spin | Required for some jackpots |
| **Coin-in** | Total amount wagered (casino metric) | Host/internal language |
| **Handle pull** | Single play on a slot | Legacy term, still used |
| **Hot machine** | Machine perceived to be paying well | Gambler's fallacy, but agents must not correct |

### Sports Betting Slang
| Term | Meaning | Context |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Degen** | Degenerate gambler; someone who bets recklessly | Can be self-deprecating badge of honor or genuine concern |
| **Chalk** | The favorite | "Taking the chalk tonight" |
| **Dog/Pup** | Underdog | "Riding the dog" |
| **Parlay** | Multi-bet combo; all must win | "Hit a 5-leg parlay" |
| **Vig/Juice** | Commission taken by the book | "What's the vig on that?" |
| **Sharp** | Professional/skilled bettor | Opposite of "square" (recreational) |
| **Square** | Recreational/amateur bettor | Not pejorative in context |
| **Run bad/Run good** | Extended streak of losses/wins | "I've been running bad all month" |
| **Steam** | Line moving rapidly due to heavy betting | "There's steam on the Lakers" |

### Casino Host / VIP Industry Terms
| Term | Meaning | Context |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Whale** | Extremely high-value player ($1M+ action) | Agent must recognize and prioritize |
| **RFB** | Room, Food, Beverage (full comp package) | "Am I RFB this trip?" |
| **ADT** | Average Daily Theoretical (expected daily loss) | How hosts value players; $500+ ADT = significant |
| **Theo** | Theoretical win (casino's expected profit from player) | "What's my theo?" = player knows the system |
| **Comp** | Complimentary benefit (room, meal, show, etc.) | Most common VIP-host word |
| **Action** | Total amount wagered | "I'll bring a lot of action this weekend" |
| **Rated play** | Play tracked by the casino for comp calculations | "Am I being rated?" |
| **Dead chip** | Non-cashable promotional chip | Common in Asian markets |
| **Lossback/Rebate** | Percentage of losses returned to player | "What's my lossback?" |
| **Trip report** | Casino's summary of a player's session | Internal but VIPs ask about it |
| **Credit line** | Casino-issued gambling credit | "I need to increase my line" |
| **Front money** | Cash deposited at the cage for play | Alternative to markers |

## 2. Generational Differences in Gambling Language

### Baby Boomer VIPs (Born 1946-1964)
**Communication style**: Formal, phone-preferred, expects personal relationship with host
**Characteristic language**:
- "I've been coming here for twenty years"
- Uses traditional terms: "marker," "credit line," "comp," "host"
- Expects host to "know" them without being told
- Table game focused (blackjack, craps, baccarat)
- "What can you do for me?" (expects negotiation)
- Dislikes being "treated like a number"
- Uses full sentences in text, proper punctuation
- May call instead of text: "Have my girl call your girl"

**Key signals for agent**: Loyalty language ("always stayed here"), expectation of reciprocity, formal tone, mentions of specific hosts by name.

### Gen X VIPs (Born 1965-1980)
**Communication style**: Mix of formal and casual, comfortable with text and email
**Characteristic language**:
- "What's my status?" (tier-conscious)
- Understands comp math: "I know my theo"
- Poker terminology crosses into general conversation
- "I'm due" (gambler's fallacy but widespread)
- References specific games and machines by name
- Text style: Complete sentences but informal, occasional abbreviations

**Key signals for agent**: Analytical language, questions about comp calculation, loyalty tier awareness, poker vocabulary spillover.

### Millennial VIPs (Born 1981-1996)
**Communication style**: Text-first, casual, expects instant response
**Characteristic language**:
- "Degen" (self-identifying, ironic badge)
- "Grinding" (sustained play, from poker/gaming culture)
- "Shipped it" (won big, from poker tournament culture)
- "EV" (expected value — understands math)
- "Nit" (tight/conservative player — pejorative)
- Sports betting vocabulary dominant: "parlay," "fade," "hammer"
- Crypto-adjacent: "whale," "diamond hands," "HODL" (borrowed from crypto into gambling)
- Abbreviations: "lmk" (let me know), "tbh" (to be honest), "ngl" (not gonna lie)
- "Can you hook me up?" (casual comp request)

**Key signals for agent**: Informal tone, crypto/gaming crossover vocabulary, comfort with numbers/math, group trip coordination, experience-seeking over pure comps.

### Gen Z Gamblers (Born 1997-2012)
**Communication style**: Ultra-casual, meme-influenced, mobile-first
**Characteristic language**:
- "Bussin'" (something is great)
- "No cap" (no lie / genuinely)
- "That's fire" (impressive)
- "Bet" (agreement/affirmation — ironic given gambling context)
- "Lowkey/Highkey" (somewhat/very)
- "Send it" (go all-in, take the risk)
- "Rizz" (charisma — may apply to dealers or hosts)
- Heavy emoji use in texts
- Voice notes over typing
- TikTok-influenced vocabulary: "POV: your casino host texts you back"

**Key signals for agent**: Short messages, emoji-heavy, slang that changes rapidly, experience/content-creation focus, group dynamics.

## 3. Cultural and Ethnic Language Variations

### Asian (Particularly Chinese/Vietnamese) Baccarat Culture
- **"Monkey"**: Face card or ten (critical baccarat term, from Cantonese)
- **"Squeeze"**: Slowly revealing cards in baccarat (ritual, not just mechanics)
- **Numbers superstitions**: 8 = lucky, 4 = death/unlucky. Room numbers, chip counts matter
- **"Dragon/Tiger"**: Side bets and game variants
- **"Pattern recognition"**: Following "roads" (bead plate, big road, big eye boy) — NOT gambler's fallacy to the player
- **Red**: Lucky color. Red clothing, red envelopes for tips
- **Group play dynamics**: Often gamble in family/clan groups, single member may handle host communication
- **Communication**: May prefer Mandarin/Cantonese text, or highly formal English
- **Gift-giving**: Cultural expectations around relationship (guanxi)
- Agent must NEVER dismiss pattern-following or superstitions — these are deeply cultural

### Hispanic/Latino Casino Culture
- **Family-oriented visits**: Multigenerational trips common
- **"Suerte"** (luck): Central concept
- **Slot-focused**: Higher proportion of slots play
- **"Maquinita"**: Slot machine (colloquial)
- **Bilingual texting**: Code-switching mid-sentence ("Can you hold a room para este weekend?")
- **Group dining**: Large party restaurant reservations common
- **Communication**: Warm, relationship-oriented, expects personal interest
- **Holidays**: Cinco de Mayo, Dia de los Muertos — casino events around these
- Agent should be comfortable with bilingual messages and not force English-only

### African American Casino Culture
- **Slots and table games**: Mix varies by region
- **Social gambling**: Casino as entertainment venue, not just gambling
- **"Hit"**: Won (universal but heavily used)
- **"Come up"**: Win big, improve situation
- **Card room culture**: Poker home game terminology crosses over
- **Church group trips**: Organized bus trips to casinos, common in Southeast
- **Entertainment focus**: Shows, concerts, dining as much as gambling
- Agent should emphasize entertainment offerings alongside gaming

### Korean/Japanese High-Roller Culture
- **Junket operators**: Intermediaries who bring high-rollers, especially from Korea
- **"Pachinko"**: Japanese gaming context (not directly casino but cultural bridge)
- **Privacy-focused**: Less likely to share personal details freely
- **Status markers**: Brand preferences, suite specifications
- **Communication**: More formal, hierarchy-aware

## 4. Regional Slang Variations

### Las Vegas
- "The Strip" (Las Vegas Boulevard resort corridor)
- "Downtown" (Fremont Street area, perceived as grittier/more authentic)
- "Locals casino" (Station, Boyd properties — for residents, not tourists)
- "Deuce" ($2 tip, minimum tip culture)
- "George/Tom" (good tipper/bad tipper among dealers)
- Informal, anything-goes atmosphere reflected in language

### Atlantic City
- "The Boardwalk" (main casino strip)
- "Jitney" (local shuttle bus)
- East Coast formality carries over — slightly more buttoned-up than Vegas
- "Total Rewards" (now Caesars Rewards, but old-timers still say it)
- Sports betting integration more prominent (NJ was early legal market)

### Tribal Casinos
- More community-oriented language
- "Sovereign nation" awareness (legal/regulatory differences)
- Regional/rural slang depending on location
- Less VIP-host culture, more floor-based interaction
- "Players club" more common than "VIP host"

### Gulf Coast (MS, LA)
- "Riverboat" (legacy term even for land-based)
- Southern hospitality expectations in service
- "Honey," "sugar," "baby" — terms of endearment in host communication are expected, not inappropriate
- Food/restaurant focus strong (New Orleans cuisine influence)

## 5. Drunk/Informal Texting Patterns in Host-Player SMS

From X/Twitter social data and casino forum discussions, real patterns observed:

### Common Abbreviations
- **"u" / "ur"**: you / your
- **"r u there"**: Are you available?
- **"wats my comp"**: What's my complimentary balance?
- **"can u get me a room"**: Room request
- **"lmk"**: Let me know
- **"thx" / "ty"**: Thanks / thank you
- **"nvm"**: Never mind
- **"omw"**: On my way
- **"eta"**: Estimated time of arrival
- **"tmrw"**: Tomorrow
- **"rn"**: Right now
- **"w/"**: With
- **"b/c"**: Because

### Drunk/Late-Night Patterns
- Multiple exclamation marks: "I just hit!!!!!"
- ALL CAPS: "I NEED A ROOM NOW"
- Typos increase: "can u getme a sute" (suite)
- Emoji-heavy: slot machine, money bag, fire, 100
- Short fragments: "room?" "dinner?" "comp me"
- Time-insensitive messaging (2-4 AM texts)
- Emotional swings: "I love this place!!!" → (30 min later) → "this casino is rigged"
- Run-on messages without punctuation
- Voice-to-text artifacts: "I need a room period can you help me period"

## 7. Implications for Hey Seven Agent

### Must-Have Language Capabilities
1. **Recognize all core VIP terms** (RFB, ADT, theo, comp, marker, action, lossback) without asking for clarification — these indicate a knowledgeable player who expects a knowledgeable host
2. **Detect "on tilt" indicators**: Emotional language, loss mentions, escalating frustration, time-of-day context. Route to empathetic response, never upsell during tilt
3. **Handle drunk/informal texting**: Parse abbreviations, typos, ALL CAPS, emoji-heavy messages without condescension
4. **Adapt tone by generation**: Formal with Boomers, analytical with Gen X, casual with Millennials, ultra-brief with Gen Z
5. **Respect cultural gambling norms**: Never dismiss superstitions (especially Asian baccarat patterns), handle bilingual messages gracefully, understand family/group dynamics

### Language the Agent Should Use
- Mirror the player's register (formal with formal, casual with casual)
- Use "comp" naturally, not "complimentary benefit"
- Know the difference between "What's my theo?" (sophisticated) and "Can I get a free room?" (casual)
- Never use gambling jargon the player hasn't used first (avoid seeming like a textbook)
- Use "action" and "play" naturally in context

### Language the Agent Should Never Use
- Never say "gambler's fallacy" or correct superstitions
- Never use clinical addiction terminology unprompted
- Never use "whale" to a player's face (internal term only)
- Never use "degen" unless the player self-identifies
- Never dismiss a loss with "that's gambling" or similar platitudes

### Guardrail Implications
- "On tilt" detection should trigger the empathy/frustration path, not standard service
- "Marker" and "credit line" mentions require extra compliance awareness (financial product)
- Responsible gaming keywords in any language/slang variant must still trigger guardrails
- Cultural terms for problem gambling may differ: "degenerado" (Spanish), problem gambling euphemisms vary by culture
